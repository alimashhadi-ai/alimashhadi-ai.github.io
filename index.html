In Progress

Library

Paper: Traffic estimation in work zones using a custom regression model and data augmentation

Accurately estimating traffic in work zones is challenging due to sparse continuous count stations. This study introduces a regularized regression model enhanced with Variational Autoencoders (VAE) to generate synthetic data and bridge sensor gaps. The approach delivers significantly improved volume estimates and outperforms benchmark models, offering a scalable solution for data-limited environments.

Paper: A hybrid framework for predicting crash severity in construction work zones using knowledge distillation and conditional GANs

Work zone crashes are rare but severe, making accurate prediction difficult. This research combines conditional GANs and knowledge distillation to predict crash severity with higher accuracy and lower computational demand. The framework effectively captures complex patterns in limited datasets, supporting faster and more reliable safety analytics in construction zones.

Paper: A GAN-augmented CNN approach for automated roadside safety assessment of rural roadways

Rural roadways face unique safety challenges from roadside features like slopes and guardrails. This work proposes a deep learning framework using GANs for data augmentation and CNNs for automated feature detection. The models align with FHWA safety standards, achieving more consistent and scalable roadside safety evaluations compared to manual inspection.

Paper: Highway asset and pavement condition management using mobile photogrammetry

Monitoring highway assets is essential but costly when using LiDAR. This study demonstrates the power of mobile photogrammetry as a low-cost, image-based alternative for highway and pavement condition assessment. The approach provides accurate asset inventories while making large-scale condition monitoring accessible to more transportation agencies.

Report: Asset Condition Assessment Using AI and Computer Vision

UDOT’s manual asset inspections are time-intensive and inconsistent. This project leverages AI and computer vision—using YOLO models and Vision-Language Models (VLMs)—to automate condition assessments of guardrails, barriers, and curbs. The system enhances maintenance prioritization, improves accuracy, and demonstrates the next generation of infrastructure monitoring powered by AI.

Report: Construction Work Zone Safety: Spatio-Temporal Analysis of Construction Work Zone Crashes

This report provides a nationwide view of work zone crash patterns, integrating survey feedback from 24 DOTs with analyses of crash severity, speed, and countermeasures. It highlights where interventions like rumble strips succeed or fall short and offers actionable recommendations to improve safety in construction environments through data-driven strategies.

Report: Automated safety assessment of rural roadways using computer vision

Manual safety assessments are slow and subjective. This report presents a computer vision framework for automatically rating rural roads per FHWA standards using Mandli imagery and machine learning. The generated safety maps help engineers identify high-risk areas and prioritize improvements, offering a scalable tool for proactive crash prevention.
